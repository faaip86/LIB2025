What's Cooking Recipes About

I’ve spent most of my life in the kitchen, and for years, the fast-paced world of restaurants was my home. I started my culinary journey in Ottawa at a cozy spot called Town, where I learned the ins and outs of running a kitchen. From there, I had the incredible opportunity to help open Corner Peach and its sister restaurant, Dukes, working alongside talented chefs and passionate people. Cooking professionally was demanding but deeply rewarding—it shaped me in ways I never expected.
After years of long hours and late nights, I decided it was time for a change. The restaurant industry had given me so much, but I found myself craving a different kind of challenge. That’s when I set my sights on becoming a Library and Information Technician The idea of organizing, preserving, and sharing knowledge in a new way excited me. It felt like a natural shift—after all, food and books both have a way of bringing people together.
Going back to school as a mature student has been an adventure of its own. It’s a different pace from the kitchen, but I’ve loved diving into the world of information management. Studying at Algonquin College has given me a fresh perspective, and I’m eager to apply my organizational skills in a new field. Libraries, much like kitchens, thrive on efficiency, creativity, and a deep respect for craft.
That said, cooking is still very much a part of my life. Now that I’m no longer working in a professional kitchen, I’ve rediscovered the joy of cooking at home. There’s something special about preparing a meal for friends and family, without the pressure of a dinner rush. I take my time, experiment with new recipes, and savor the process in a way I couldn’t when I was cooking for a packed restaurant.
Food has always been about connection for me, and that hasn’t changed. Whether I’m making a simple pasta dish or slow-braising something special, the best part is gathering around the table with people I care about. I love introducing friends to new flavors, swapping stories over a home-cooked meal, and seeing the joy that good food brings.
Though my career path has shifted, I carry my experiences as a chef with me every day. The discipline, creativity, and attention to detail I honed in the kitchen continue to serve me well in my studies. I see my time in restaurants as an incredible chapter in my life—one that has led me to where I am now, embracing a new passion with the same dedication.
As I look ahead, I’m excited about the possibilities. Whether it’s in a library, an archive, or a research setting, I know I’ll find fulfillment in this next stage. But no matter where my career takes me, one thing is certain: my love for food, cooking, and sharing meals will always be a central part of who I am.
