Sesame Miso Cookies

Photo by David Malosh for The New York Times

Prep Time: 30 mins, plus chilling

Cook Time: 12-15 mins

Servings: 12 cookies

The picture for this recipe is actually from NYT Cooking, but this recipe was actually the inspiration for these Sesame Miso cookies. I wanted something with a more pronounced savoriness so I landed on tahini instead of peanut butter. For a better look at the actual cookies, check out this video from the Corner Peach Instagram page. These cookies are salty and sweet, crunchy and chewy. Made with tahini, miso, brown sugar, and a ton of sesame seeds, they have a toasty, nutty flavour without being overly sweet. Great dipped in coffee!

Ingredients


  • 675g AP flour
  • 12g baking soda
  • 8g baking powder
  • 345g soft butter
  • 660g brown sugar
  • 300g sugar
  • 240g miso
  • 240g tahini
  • 3 eggs
  • 20g vanilla
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • Sesame seeds for rolling

Instructions


  1. In a bowl, combine flour, baking soda and baking powder, and whisk until incorporated. Set aside.

  2. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, cream butter, light brown sugar and granulated sugar at medium speed until light and fluffy, about 3-5 minutes.

  3. Add the miso, tahini, eggs, vanilla, and lemon zest and mix until incorporated, scraping down the sides and bottom making sure everything is thoroughly mixed.

  4. Add the dry ingredients and mix on low until just incorporated. Try not to overmix the dough

  5. Using a 2oz scoop portion the cookie dough into balls on a parchment lined baking sheet.

  6. Chill balls of cookie dough for 30 minutes so they are easier to handle.

  7. Once cookie dough is slightly chilled roll balls in sesame seeds and put back on baking sheet. Chill for 1 hour (or overnight) so butter solidifies. At this point you can also freeze the dough and bake when desired. Freeze on a sheet tray, then transfer the dough balls into a freezer bag. They’ll keep for up to 3 months. When ready, bake straight from frozen.

  8. When ready to bake, heat oven to 350°F. Bake for 12-15 minutes, spining halfway. Bake until edges begin to crisp, but the middle is just set.

  9. Allow cookies to cool on baking sheet. Once fully cooled store cookies in an airtight container.
